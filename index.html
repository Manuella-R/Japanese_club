2024 Highlights

2024 has been one for the books! We organized exciting new activities, eco-friendly camping initiatives, and hosted various community-building events. Thank you to all who joined us in making these moments unforgettable!

April

In April, the Japanese Club held weekly meetings every Friday, where members engaged in a variety of fun and educational activities. We kicked off with exciting anime Kahoots, where participants tested their anime knowledge in a lively quiz format. We also took our first steps in learning basic Japanese greetings and explored the art of origami.

May

In May, we hosted an enriching online webinar with Diana, who connected us to two prestigious Japanese schools in Tokyo. Diana provided valuable insights into living and studying in Japan, including the visa process, available visa types, the job market, and her personal experiences in Japan. The principals of the two schools also joined the session to discuss the learning experiences at their institutions.

June

June brought an exciting opportunity for our members as we attended the International Cultural Festival at the Nairobi Museum, where we experienced not only Japanese culture but also a variety of other cultures from around the world. We also continued with our regular Friday meetings, fostering a balance between learning and fun.

July

In July, the Japanese Club visited the Japan Embassy, where we learned about special parts of Japanese culture through embassy video essays. Members practiced calligraphy, wrote their names in Japanese, wore traditional Japanese clothing, and learned how to make origami cranes and frogs.

September

September was an eventful month! We kicked off with a Club Exposure event where the Japan Embassy partnered with us to bring authentic Japanese food displays to Strathmore. This event also featured educational talks on scholarships and allowed attendees to try on traditional Japanese attire. The Chinese Club joined us for a team-building event at Karura Forest, where we enjoyed a fun-filled 21 km walk. The month concluded with a trip to the Japan Embassy for the Short Movie Festival, where we watched a Japanese comedy film.

October

October was a milestone month as we secured a partnership with ChiQ Restaurant and began plans to bring their authentic Japanese food to Strathmore. We also met with foreign exchange students from Akita University and went on a scenic hike at Karura Forest. Our weekly meetings continued, and members attended the second day of the Japan Embassy's 3-day Movie Festival, enjoying a screening and tasting tofu after the second film. Later in the month, a group of us attended the Halloween party hosted by ChiQ Restaurant at Sarit Center.

November

In November, we hosted a Language Week in partnership with Sticker Hive, where we sold unique Japanese-themed stickers on Wednesday and Thursday. On Thursday, the Japan Embassy joined us for an interactive cultural experience, featuring activities like calligraphy, origami, trying on traditional clothing, and playing Japanese games. It was a fantastic way for members and newcomers alike to immerse themselves in Japanese culture!

Crying Sumo Contest

Every year, a unique event takes place in Japan called the "Crying Sumo" contest. The goal is for sumo wrestlers to make babies cry. The tradition is believed to bring good health and good fortune to the babies!

Punctual Trains

Japanese trains are incredibly punctual! The average delay of the Shinkansen (bullet train) is only 54 seconds per year. Train staff even apologize for delays of just 20 seconds!

Slurping is Polite

Slurping your noodles in Japan is considered polite! It shows appreciation for the food and helps cool down hot noodles. The louder, the better!

Convenience Stores Everywhere

Japan has more than 55,000 convenience stores! These 24/7 shops sell everything from fresh food to concert tickets, and they're a vital part of daily life.

Cherry Blossom Forecast

Japan has an official cherry blossom forecast! The "Sakura Zensen" predicts when cherry blossoms will bloom across the country, and people plan their viewing parties accordingly.
